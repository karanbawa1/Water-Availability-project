Primary Water Sources in Your Area

  • Rivers and Streams: These are natural sources providing fresh water, often used for community needs and agricultural irrigation.
  • Lakes and Reservoirs: Commonly used for drinking water and recreational activities, often regulated to maintain water levels.
  • Groundwater: Includes wells and aquifers; essential for regions lacking surface water sources.

Maintaining Clean Water Sources

  • Avoid disposing of chemicals or trash near rivers and lakes.
  • Support conservation efforts to protect wetlands and other natural water reservoirs.
  • Get involved in local clean-up drives to help preserve water quality.

Benefits of Local Water Sources

  • Provides drinking water for communities and wildlife.
  • Helps maintain biodiversity and natural ecosystems.
  • Supports agricultural and industrial needs in the region.

Learn about your local water sources to help keep them clean and sustainable.
