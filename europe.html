Overview

Many parts of Europe are currently well provided with fresh water but it is unevenly distributed between and within countries, and there are shortages in a number of areas.

Main Water Sources

  • Rivers: Danube, Rhine, and Volga.
  • Lakes: Lake Geneva, Lake Baikal.
  • Groundwater: Widely used in agricultural and drinking water supplies.

Key Challenges

  • Climate Change: Increasing droughts and heatwaves.
  • Water Pollution: Industrial and agricultural runoff in rivers and lakes.
  • Seasonal Variability: Some southern regions face summer water shortages.

Conservation Initiatives

Europe is investing in water recycling, sustainable farming practices, and cross-border water management policies.
