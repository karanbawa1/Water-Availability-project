Water Availability in Oceania

Overview

In the context of Oceania, the lack of safely managed water services is even more pronounced where only 57% of the population have access to basic drinking water facilities.

Main Water Sources

  • Rivers and Reservoirs: Murray-Darling Basin in Australia, water catchments in New Zealand.
  • Rainwater: Rainwater collection is vital for many Pacific islands.
  • Desalination: Coastal areas increasingly rely on desalination.

Key Challenges

  • Climate Variability: Frequent droughts and cyclones.
  • Limited Freshwater: Small islands have very limited freshwater resources.
  • Environmental Degradation: Agriculture and mining impact water quality in Australia.

Conservation Initiatives

Efforts include desalination projects, rainwater harvesting, and water-saving technologies in agriculture.
