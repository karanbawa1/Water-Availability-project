Overview

In the United States, even 60% of all water used for irrigation purposes comes from groundwater resources. This dependency on groundwater, however, comes with its risks as well..

Main Water Sources

  • Rivers and Lakes: Mississippi River, Great Lakes, Colorado River.
  • Groundwater: Ogallala Aquifer, other regional aquifers.
  • Rainfall: Seasonal and geographical variations across the continent.

Key Challenges

  • Groundwater Depletion: Overuse of aquifers, especially in agricultural regions.
  • Pollution: Industrial waste, agricultural runoff, and urban pollution.
  • Climate Change: Droughts and temperature changes affect water availability.

Conservation Initiatives

Efforts are underway to promote sustainable water usage, including initiatives such as:

  • Water Recycling: Municipal and industrial wastewater recycling.
  • Desalination: Coastal desalination plants for cities like San Diego.
  • Improved Irrigation Techniques: Efficient water use in agricultural areas.
