Overview

Africa is the worldÂ´s second-driest continent after Australia. About 66% of Africa is arid or semi-arid and more than 300 of the 800 million people in sub-Saharan Africa live in a water-scarce environment meaning that they have less than 1,000 m3 per capita per year.

Main Water Sources

  • Rivers: Nile, Congo, and Niger rivers.
  • Groundwater: Essential for arid regions.
  • Seasonal Rainfall: Key source but highly variable.

Key Challenges

  • Infrastructure: Limited water infrastructure and sanitation.
  • Climate Change: Increased drought and desertification.
  • Political Factors: Conflicts over water rights.

Conservation Initiatives

Efforts include building reservoirs, improving water access, and promoting efficient irrigation practices.
